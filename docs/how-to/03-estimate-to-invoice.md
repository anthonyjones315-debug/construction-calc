# 03 Estimate To Invoice

Use this guide when you want to move from a fresh calculation to a saved estimate, then all the way through to a client-facing PDF and invoice workflow.

## Goal

Turn field math into a clean client document without retyping the job more than necessary.

## Step 1: Start from the right calculator

### Action

Open `Calculators` or `Command Center`, choose the trade tool you need, and enter the job inputs.

### Context

The app is designed to start with trade math first. The estimate workflow is strongest when the numbers come directly from the calculator instead of being typed in later from memory.

### Verification

Make sure the result card shows the quantity, pricing, or total you expect before you save anything.

## Step 2: Review the result before saving

### Action

Check the main result, the secondary numbers, and the material list. If needed, adjust waste, pricing, or dimensions before you continue.

### Context

This is your clean-up moment. It is much faster to correct a dimension now than after the estimate has already been saved and shared.

### Verification

If the result would be safe to hand to a supplier or client, move on. If not, fix the inputs first.

## Step 3: Save the estimate

### Action

Use `Save Estimate` from the calculator workflow.

### Context

Saving creates the working record that later powers the saved estimate view, the financial dashboard, and downstream PDF workflows.

### Verification

Open `Saved Estimates` and confirm the estimate appears in the list with the correct name and status.

## Step 4: Generate the estimate PDF

### Action

From the calculator flow, use `Finalize & Send` and choose `Download PDF`, or open the saved estimate list and use the `PDF` action there.

### Context

This is the fastest way to create a client-ready estimate document from the same math you just reviewed.

### Verification

Open the PDF and confirm:

- The estimate name is correct
- The totals match the saved estimate
- The county and tax language make sense for the job

## Step 5: Open the saved estimate detail for billing

### Action

Open the saved estimate record and move to the progress billing area when you are ready to invoice.

### Context

This is where contract total, billed amount, remaining balance, and invoice records stay connected.

### Verification

Confirm you can see:

- `Contract`
- `Billed`
- `Remaining`
- The `Add Invoice` button

## Step 6: Create the invoice record

### Action

Click `Add Invoice`, then fill in:

- Invoice number
- Amount
- Issued date
- Due date
- Status
- Payment method
- Payment instructions

### Context

This keeps the invoice tied directly to the estimate instead of creating a separate disconnected document.

### Verification

After entry, check that the `Billed` and `Remaining` numbers update correctly on the same screen.

## Step 7: Send or share the invoice PDF

### Action

Use one of these actions on the invoice row:

- `Download Invoice PDF`
- `Email Invoice`
- `Share Invoice` on mobile

### Context

These actions use the same saved billing record, so the invoice PDF should match the invoice line you just created.

### Verification

Open the invoice PDF and confirm:

- Invoice number matches
- Amount matches
- Client and contractor information look right
- Payment instructions are present if needed

## Workflow done when

You are done when all of the following are true:

- The original calculator math was reviewed before saving
- The estimate appears in `Saved Estimates`
- The estimate PDF matches the saved record
- The invoice record updates billed and remaining totals correctly
- The invoice PDF is ready to send to the client
